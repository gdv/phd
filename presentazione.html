[cite_start]What is a PhD? [cite: 264]

  • [cite_start]A PhD is an advanced research program (highest level of academic qualification) that possibly results in the attainment of the Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degree. [cite: 265]
  • The primary goal of a phd is the production of new knowledge through original research. [cite: 266]
  • [cite_start]PhD students receive training along several dimensions including research methods, technical training, interdisciplinary training, RRI (Responsible Research and Innovation) Training, career development, and soft skills training. [cite: 267]

[cite_start]PhD Structure [cite: 284]

  • [cite_start]Duration: 3 years [cite: 285]
  • Coursework: Advanced courses and seminars during the first 1-2 years (4 disciplinary courses + 2-3 interdisciplinary courses). [cite: 287]
  • [cite_start]Research: Development of a research project under the guidance of a faculty supervisor. [cite: 290]
  • [cite_start]Publications: New knowledge is disseminated mainly through the publication of scientific papers. [cite: 313]
  • Doctoral Thesis: The thesis aims to summarize the PhD journey and must be defended by an expert committee. [cite: 293]

The Path [cite: 324]

Every year there is a checkpoint of the advances [cite: 324]

  • [cite_start]1st year: Definition of the problem and knowledge of the state of the art. [cite: 325]
  • [cite_start]2nd year: Intermediate results and expected final contribution. [cite: 326]
  • 3rd year: Strong evidence of the relevance of the contribution. [cite: 327]

A day in the life of a PhD [cite: 351]

  • [cite_start]You start by doing some research on the topics you are most interested in. [cite: 352]
  • [cite_start]You and your supervisor(s) study how to improve the state-of-the-art with new solutions. [cite: 353]
  • The experiment phase starts: a lot of experiments, ideas, advice and different results. [cite: 354]
  • [cite_start]Eventually, you find something interesting/worth writing about and summarize everything into a paper. [cite: 355]
  • [cite_start]You send the paper to a conference/journal for publication and stay in touch for the peer review process. [cite: 356]

[cite_start]Additional Information [cite: 358]

  • [cite_start]Period abroad: Each student is expected to complete a 6 months period abroad. [cite: 359]
  • PhD Scholarship: PhD students have a scholarship of about €1,200/month. [cite: 360]
  • [cite_start]Extra income is allowed through extra activities (internal or external) as long as the year income is not doubled and the activity does not interfere with the phd path. [cite: 361]
  • [cite_start]PhD students are assigned with a budget to travel, attend summer school, conferences, etc. [cite: 362]
  • Several community activities: phd talks, phd colloquia, phd day. [cite: 363]

The Selection Process [cite: 392]

  • [cite_start]Every year there is an open call (normally around April). [cite: 393, 394]
  • [cite_start]Candidates apply by submitting their CV and a short project proposal. [cite: 395]
  • A committee assesses the CV, the projects, and interviews the candidates. [cite: 398]
  • [cite_start]Final ranked list is created. [cite: 399]
  • [cite_start]A new cycle with the new students starts every year in November. [cite: 400]

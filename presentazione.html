What is a PhD?

  • A PhD is an advanced research program (highest level of academic qualification) that possibly results in the attainment of the Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degree.
  • The primary goal of a phd is the production of new knowledge through original research.
  • PhD students receive training along several dimensions including research methods, technical training, interdisciplinary training, RRI (Responsible Research and Innovation) Training, career development, and soft skills training.

PhD Structure

  • Duration: 3 years
  • Coursework: Advanced courses and seminars during the first 1-2 years (4 disciplinary courses + 2-3 interdisciplinary courses).
  • Research: Development of a research project under the guidance of a faculty supervisor.
  • Publications: New knowledge is disseminated mainly through the publication of scientific papers.
  • Doctoral Thesis: The thesis aims to summarize the PhD journey and must be defended by an expert committee.

The Path

Every year there is a checkpoint of the advances

  • 1st year: Definition of the problem and knowledge of the state of the art.
  • 2nd year: Intermediate results and expected final contribution.
  • 3rd year: Strong evidence of the relevance of the contribution.

A day in the life of a PhD

  • You start by doing some research on the topics you are most interested in.
  • You and your supervisor(s) study how to improve the state-of-the-art with new solutions.
  • The experiment phase starts: a lot of experiments, ideas, advice and different results.
  • Eventually, you find something interesting/worth writing about and summarize everything into a paper.
  • You send the paper to a conference/journal for publication and stay in touch for the peer review process.

Additional Information

  • Period abroad: Each student is expected to complete a 6 months period abroad.
  • PhD Scholarship: PhD students have a scholarship of about €1,200/month.
  • Extra income is allowed through extra activities (internal or external) as long as the year income is not doubled and the activity does not interfere with the phd path.
  • PhD students are assigned with a budget to travel, attend summer school, conferences, etc.
  • Several community activities: phd talks, phd colloquia, phd day.

Key Abilities Developed

  • Independence
  • Critical thinking and problem solving
  • Adaptability and Resiliency
  • Analytical and technical skills
  • Interdisciplinary understanding
  • Capability to lead research and Innovation
  • Communication skills
  • Collaboration

A good PhD candidate should be…

  • Curious, eager to learn.
  • Motivated in exploring new frontiers of knowledge.
  • Aimed at improving the specialisation of knowledge and skills in a given area.
  • Interested in becoming an internationally-recognized expert in a field.
  • Interested in developing an international experience, collaborating globally.
  • Interested in building a professional network within academia and industry.

A bad PhD candidate…

  • Only wants to postpone the date he/she will be entering into the job market.
  • Is convinced that if he/she does a phd he/she will for sure become a professor.
  • Is merely interested in continuing the work done during the thesis.
  • Thinks the phd is only a way to complete the “educational path”.

The Selection Process

  • Every year there is an open call (normally around April).
  • Candidates apply by submitting their CV and a short project proposal.
  • A committee assesses the CV, the projects, and interviews the candidates.
  • Final ranked list is created.
  • A new cycle with the new students starts every year in November.

Post-PhD: Career Opportunities

  • Level of satisfaction: 9 (on a 9-10 scale).

Post-PhD: Career Paths

  • 👩🏻‍🏫 Academic Career: Professor, researcher, contract professor.
  • 🔬 Research Centers: Researcher, project manager.
  • 🌉 Large Corporations: Research scientist, research engineer, innovation manager.
  • 💡 Start-Ups: CTO, lead researcher, innovation strategist.
  • 💸 Entrepreneurship: Starting your own company based on research conducted during the PhD.

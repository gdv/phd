What is a PhD?

  • Advanced research program
  • Production of new knowledge through original research.
  • Training on research methods, technical training, interdisciplinary training, Responsible Research and Innovation, career development, and soft skills training.

PhD Structure

  • Duration: 3 years
  • Coursework: 4 CS courses, + 2-3 interdisciplinary courses, overall 11 CFU
  • Research: A lot!
  • Publications: Main dissemination vehicle
  • Doctoral Thesis: Summary of the PhD journey. Defended against an expert committee.

The Path

Every year there is a checkpoint of the advances

  • 1st year: Definition of the problem and knowledge of the state of the art
  • 2nd year: Intermediate results and definition of expected outcome
  • 3rd year: Main contribution

A day in the life of a PhD

  • You start by doing some research on the topics you are most interested in.
  • You and your supervisor(s) study how to improve the state-of-the-art with new solutions.
  • The experiment phase starts: a lot of experiments, ideas, advice and different results.
  • Eventually, you find something interesting/worth writing about and summarize everything into a paper.
  • You send the paper to a conference/journal for publication and stay in touch for the peer review process.

Additional Information

  • Period abroad: 6 months working abroad
  • PhD Scholarship: approx €1,200/month.
  • Research budget: approx €1,600/year plus supervisor funds.
  • Activities: phd talks, phd colloquia, phd day.

Key Abilities Developed

  • Independence
  • Critical thinking and problem solving
  • Adaptability and Resiliency
  • Analytical and technical skills
  • Interdisciplinary understanding
  • Capability to lead research and Innovation
  • Communication skills
  • Collaboration

A good PhD candidate should be…

  • Curious, eager to learn.
  • Motivated in exploring new frontiers of knowledge.
  • Aimed at improving the specialisation of knowledge and skills in a given area.
  • Interested in becoming an internationally-recognized expert in a field.
  • Interested in developing an international experience, collaborating globally.
  • Interested in building a professional network within academia and industry.

A bad PhD candidate…

  • Only wants to postpone the date he/she will be entering into the job market.
  • Is convinced that if he/she does a phd he/she will for sure become a professor.
  • Is merely interested in continuing the work done during the thesis.
  • Thinks the phd is only a way to complete the “educational path”.

The Selection Process

  • Every year there is an open call (normally around April).
  • Candidates apply by submitting their CV and a short project proposal.
  • A committee assesses the CV, the projects, and interviews the candidates.
  • Final ranked list is created.
  • A new cycle with the new students starts every year in November.

Post-PhD: Career Opportunities

Cycle XXXIV (AlmaLaurea)

  • Employment: 100% of respondents are working.
  • Area of Employment: 50% private sector, 50% public sector.
  • Extent of doing research: 75% to a high extent, 25% to a low extent.
  • Level of satisfaction: 9 (on a 9-10 scale).

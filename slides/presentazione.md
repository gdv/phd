## What is a PhD? [cite: 264]

* A PhD is an advanced research program (highest level of academic qualification) that possibly results in the attainment of the Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degree. [cite: 265]
* The primary goal of a phd is the production of new knowledge through original research. [cite: 266]
* PhD students receive training along several dimensions including research methods, technical training, interdisciplinary training, RRI (Responsible Research and Innovation) Training, career development, and soft skills training. [cite: 267]

## PhD Structure [cite: 284]

* **Duration:** 3 years [cite: 285]
* **Coursework:** Advanced courses and seminars during the first 1-2 years (4 disciplinary courses + 2-3 interdisciplinary courses). [cite: 287]
* **Research:** Development of a research project under the guidance of a faculty supervisor. [cite: 290]
* **Publications:** New knowledge is disseminated mainly through the publication of scientific papers. [cite: 313]
* **Doctoral Thesis:** The thesis aims to summarize the PhD journey and must be defended by an expert committee. [cite: 293]

---

## The Path [cite: 324]
*Every year there is a checkpoint of the advances* [cite: 324]

* **1st year:** Definition of the problem and knowledge of the state of the art. [cite: 325]
* **2nd year:** Intermediate results and expected final contribution. [cite: 326]
* **3rd year:** Strong evidence of the relevance of the contribution. [cite: 327]

---

## A day in the life of a PhD [cite: 351]

* You start by doing some research on the topics you are most interested in. [cite: 352]
* You and your supervisor(s) study how to improve the state-of-the-art with new solutions. [cite: 353]
* The experiment phase starts: a lot of experiments, ideas, advice and different results. [cite: 354]
* Eventually, you find something interesting/worth writing about and summarize everything into a paper. [cite: 355]
* You send the paper to a conference/journal for publication and stay in touch for the peer review process. [cite: 356]

## Additional Information [cite: 358]

* **Period abroad:** Each student is expected to complete a 6 months period abroad. [cite: 359]
* **PhD Scholarship:** PhD students have a scholarship of about €1,200/month. [cite: 360]
* Extra income is allowed through extra activities (internal or external) as long as the year income is not doubled and the activity does not interfere with the phd path. [cite: 361]
* PhD students are assigned with a budget to travel, attend summer school, conferences, etc. [cite: 362]
* Several community activities: phd talks, phd colloquia, phd day. [cite: 363]

## A bad PhD candidate... [cite: 387]

* Only wants to postpone the date he/she will be entering into the job market. [cite: 388]
* Is convinced that if he/she does a phd he/she will for sure become a professor. [cite: 389]
* Is merely interested in continuing the work done during the thesis. [cite: 390]
* Thinks the phd is only a way to complete the “educational path”. [cite: 391]

## The Selection Process [cite: 392]

* Every year there is an open call (normally around April). [cite: 393, 394]
* Candidates apply by submitting their CV and a short project proposal. [cite: 395]
* A committee assesses the CV, the projects, and interviews the candidates. [cite: 398]
* Final ranked list is created. [cite: 399]
* A new cycle with the new students starts every year in November. [cite: 400]
